Projects

Here are some of the projects that I have worked on or I am currently working on:


1. Neutron Stars in Modified Gravity

I worked with the Compact Object Astrophysics Group (COAST) at my college, where I explored the effects of modified gravity on the structure of neutron stars. The project focused on studying how the TOV equations are altered in alternative theories of gravity. In particular, I analyzed the case of R2 gravity by introducing an αR2 term to the Einstein–Hilbert action and examined how this modification influences the mass–radius relationship and stability of neutron stars.
This was a pretty interesting project which certainly helped broaden my horizons. Currently I am working on numerically solving the TOV equations in order to see the evolution of the Ricci Scalar outside the stellar surface. Unlike in standard GR, Birkhoff's theorem is no longer valid!!!


2. Oppenheimer-Snyder Collapse

This is an ongoing project for my General Relativity course under Dr. Ritam Mallick at IISERB. I am exploring one of the earliest physical descriptions of gravitational collapse leading to a black hole. Currently, I am working my way through Oppenheimer and Snyder’s seminal paper on the subject. I plan to write about this fascinating topic on my blog and attempt to simulate the scenario using GR1D, a one-dimensional general-relativistic simulation software.


3. Learning Numerical Relativity

This is a slightly ambitious pet project of mine. I have always been fascinated simulations of black-holes and neutron stars mergers and always wanted to give them a try myself. Currently I'm going through the first few chapters of Baumgarte's book on NR (a great book btw!! he also has a lecture series from ICTS on youtube).
